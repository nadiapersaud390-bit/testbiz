Trust / Skepticism
"Are you a fraud?" / "Does this scam really work?"
Option A — Calm & Direct

"I completely get why you'd ask that — there are a lot of bad actors out there and it's smart to be cautious. I'm just confirming basic pre-qualification details on behalf of the Business Loan Division here in California. No application, no credit pull, nothing gets submitted without your full approval. You're in control the whole time."

Option B — Flip It with Humor

"Ha — I wish! If this were a scam, I'd be calling from a beach somewhere. We're a legitimate lending division based in California. My only job right now is to check if your business qualifies for working capital programs. That's it."

Option C — Validate & Reassure

"That's honestly a fair question and I'm glad you're asking. We're the Business Loan Division — a direct private lending group. We work exclusively with licensed financial institutions. I'm not asking for any payment, bank login, or sensitive data today — just basic business details to see if you pre-qualify."

Don't get defensive. Stay calm, match their energy, and keep it moving.

Website / Online Presence
"What is the name of your website?" / "Why is there no website?"
Option A — Smooth Deflect

"Our division works through a secure internal system — we don't operate through a public-facing website since our clients come through pre-qualified referrals only. Think of it like a private network, not a retail bank. Once you're connected with the senior advisor, they can walk you through everything in detail."

Option B — Keep Focus on the Call

"We don't have a consumer website because everything we do is handled directly through verified business referrals and our partner network. You won't find us on Google like a regular lender — that's actually by design to protect our clients' information. Our senior advisors are the real resource — they can answer everything once I confirm your details."

Option C — Acknowledge & Redirect

"That's a totally fair thing to ask. We're a private lending division so we operate through partner channels rather than a public site. What I can tell you is everything is handled in California under standard business-lending regulations. The advisor you'll speak with can verify everything directly."

Don't stumble here. Say it with confidence — 'private network' and 'referral-based' sound professional, not suspicious.

Pre-Qualified but No Info
"If I'm already pre-qualified, why don't you have my information?"
Option A — Simple & Logical

"Great question. The pre-qualification is based on general business data that came through our partner network — things like your industry type and estimated revenue range. But before we can match you to a specific program, I just need to verify a few key details like your current monthly revenue and time in business. It's a quick confirmation, not a full application."

Option B — Analogy Approach

"It's kind of like when your bank pre-approves you for a credit card based on your credit file — they still ask you to confirm a few things before issuing it. We have your business flagged as a potential match, but I need to verify the current details to make sure you get the right program."

Option C — Honest & Transparent

"The pre-qualification comes from data our partners share — it tells us your business may qualify, but it doesn't give us everything. I just need to confirm your monthly revenue and how long you've been in business. Takes about two minutes and then I can connect you with a senior advisor who will have the full picture."

Say 'partner network' or 'data match' — avoid saying 'we bought a list.'

Business Identity
"What business are you calling?"
Option A — Confident Opener (have the name)

"I'm reaching out to the owner of [Business Name] — is this [Owner Name]? I'm calling because your business came up as a potential match for our working capital programs based on your revenue profile."

Option B — If You Don't Have the Name

"I'm calling based on a pre-qualification match in our system for a business in your area. I want to make sure I'm speaking with the right person — are you the owner or decision-maker for the business?"

Option C — When They Challenge

"We work from partner-sourced business data, so sometimes the specific name in our system needs to be confirmed with the owner directly — that's actually why I'm calling. Can I confirm — is this the right number for the business owner?"

Address / Physical Location
"Can I have your address so I can come in person?" / "What's your address in California?"
Option A — Security Angle

"For security reasons, we don't disclose exact branch locations publicly — that's a policy we follow to protect our clients and operations. Everything we do is fully handled remotely, which actually makes it faster and more convenient for business owners. All documents, agreements, and advisor calls are done securely over phone and email."

Option B — Professional & Smooth

"We're based in California and operate across multiple offices — we don't have a single walk-in location since we work with business owners nationwide. Everything is handled remotely through our secure process. Once the senior advisor connects with you, they can provide our official business contact information."

Option C — Redirect to Action

"I completely understand wanting to verify who you're dealing with — that's smart. We operate out of California but we're fully remote. What I can do is get you connected with the senior advisor right now, and they can send you our official contact details directly. Does that work?"

Don't say you can't give the address in a way that sounds sketchy. Tie it to 'security' and 'fully remote operations.'

Do Not Call List
"I'm on the Do Not Call list — why are you still calling?"
Option A — B2B Exemption, Calm

"I completely understand that frustration. Just to clarify — business-to-business calls like ours aren't covered under the same Do Not Call rules that apply to consumer calls. Since we're calling about business funding for your company, we're permitted to reach out. That said, if you'd like to be removed from our specific list, I can absolutely do that right now."

Option B — Acknowledge & Offer Removal

"I hear you — that's genuinely frustrating. I want to be transparent: B2B outreach like business lending calls falls under a different regulatory category than personal consumer calls. But I respect your time — would you like me to remove you from our outreach list completely? Or would it make sense to just take two minutes to see if you actually qualify?"

Option C — Soft & Empathetic

"I totally get it — nobody wants unexpected calls. Business outreach for commercial funding programs is treated differently from consumer solicitation legally, but that doesn't make it less annoying when you're busy. I'll keep it short: if your business doesn't qualify, I'll let you go immediately and remove you. Fair enough?"

Always offer to remove them. It builds trust and often makes them stay on the line.

AI / Anthony Smith Question

Option B — Redirect Smoothly

"Anthony is part of our marketing team — I'm the pre-qualification specialist who confirms your business details first. Once I verify a couple of things, I'll get you to the right person immediately. Takes about two minutes — is now still okay?"

Option C — If They Press Hard on "Is This AI?"

"I'm a real person — I handle the pre-qualification side of the process. Anthony is from our marketing team who coordinates the outreach, but all conversations and funding approvals go through real advisors like me. Would you like to continue?"

Be consistent across the whole team — always use the same answer: "Anthony is from our marketing team."
